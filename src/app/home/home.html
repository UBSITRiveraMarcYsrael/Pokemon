The World of Pokémon Regions

Kanto Region History

The Kanto region, named after the real-world Kantō region of Japan, introduced the original generation of Pokémon journeys. It is historically known for its industrialized cities like Saffron and Vermilion, contrasting with rural settings like Pallet Town. Kanto centers heavily around scientific advancements, famously housing the Pokémon Lab on Cinnabar Island and the corporate Silph Co. headquarters.

Johto Region History

Located directly west of Kanto, the Johto region leans deep into ancient mythologies, cultural heritage, and legendary traditions. Cities like Ecruteak and Violet feature historical architecture, traditional pagodas, and shrines dedicated to legendary birds and guardians. Johto is famous for its natural landmarks, Apricorn trees, and a deep spiritual connection shared between humans and nature.

Hoenn Region History

The history of the Hoenn region is a dramatic tale of environmental upheaval shaped by the primal forces of land, sea, and sky. Millions of years ago, the land-carving Groudon and ocean-expanding Kyogre fought a cataclysmic battle for regional supremacy until the sky deity, Rayquaza, descended to strip them of their powers and seal them away. Thousands of years later, a series of devastating meteor strikes created landmarks like Sootopolis City and forced the ancient Draconid people to summon Rayquaza once more, triggering the world's first Mega Evolution to subdue the awakened titans. This fragile peace held until the modern era, when the eco-terrorist factions Team Magma and Team Aqua recklessly reawakened the legendary beasts, forcing a young Trainer from Littleroot Town to intervene, restore planetary balance, and ultimately fulfill an ancient prophecy by riding Mega Rayquaza into space to destroy a world-threatening asteroid.
